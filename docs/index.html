Motivation

Algae are organisms that live in aquatic environments and produce their own nutrition through photosynthesis. While these organisms are typically benign (not harmful), there has been an increase in harmful algal blooms (HAB) which can produce harmful toxins due largely to human activities such increased carbon dioxide emissions driving climate change. For example, due to the Earth’s warming water temperature, increased precipitation, and abundance of nutrients, bacteria are able to thrive and proliferate at rates much faster than normal.

In addition, algae absorb sunlight and heat from the aquatic environment, blocking other vegetation from nutrients, leading to a positive feedback loop promoting more algae growth leading to the increase in potential harmful algal blooms.Furthermore, the varying rainfall patterns due to climate change lead to increased rainfall, causing more fertilizer runoffs, which increase nutrients in their habitats, leading to overgrowth of algae. Finally, the increase in CO2 simulates the growth rates of HABs and helps eliminate competing algae by further acidifying the aquatic ecosystem. The specific type of algae that is toxic is called Cyanobacteria. This bacteria then produces the toxin called cyanotoxins. Cyanotoxins cause extremely harmful interactions with humans when consumed, primarily resulting in conditions such as headaches, skin irritation, diarrhea, memory loss, and death.

Harmful algal blooms are an important problem because they can contaminate drinking water when filtration systems are inadequate, pose health risks to humans and animals, and damage freshwater and coastal ecosystems such as shellfish habitats. Addressing HABs can also be costly, requiring water sources to be closed disrupting local economies like fisheries and tourism. As anthropogenic climate change continues to warm water, affect precipitation and increase runoff, algal blooms are expected to become more frequent and more of a concern. This makes research into Algal bloom prediction interesting but also urgent as this issue grows further.
